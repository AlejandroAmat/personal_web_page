Advanced Skin Detection with MATLAB: A Mathematical Approach

As part of our Image Processing Laboratory project, we, Alejandro Amat and Pablo DÃ­az, under the guidance of professor Oscar Pina, embarked on developing a skin detection algorithm using MATLAB. This document outlines the mathematical and statistical models that form the backbone of our approach.

Project Objective

The primary aim is to classify image pixels into skin or non-skin categories, leveraging a dataset of 101 images for training our model. This entails the identification and analysis of pixels exhibiting skin-like properties based on color and texture.

Statistical Model for Skin Detection

Our approach uses a statistical model to differentiate skin pixels from others. The model is based on the hypothesis:

  • H0: Pixel belongs to skin class.
  • H1: Pixel does not belong to skin class.

We denote the pixel value by xi,j, where i and j are the pixel indices.

Color Space Transformation

To effectively process images, we transform them into the YCbCr color space, focusing on the Cb and Cr chrominance components. This transformation facilitates the distinction between skin and non-skin pixels:

images{i}=rgb2ycbcr(images{i})

Creating and Comparing Histograms

Two histograms are created from the training images: one for skin pixels and another for the background. These histograms help in calculating the conditional chroma probabilities, following the Bayesian decision criterion:

log(P(C|p) * P(p)) ≥ log(P(C|f) * P(f))

This equation allows us to decide if a pixel is more likely to be skin or not based on the calculated probabilities.

Evaluation Metrics

Performance is measured using Precision, Recall, and the F-S
